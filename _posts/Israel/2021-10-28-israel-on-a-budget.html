If you're planning a trip to Israel it is important to know that Israel is a relatively expensive country, but it does not have to be that way. In this post, I will give you examples of how you can save money on your trip to Israel.

 

Currency:

The currency in Israel is NIS (New Israeli Shekel). When landing in Israel you could exchange some cash at the airport but they overcharge there.

How to Save money:

Bring 200-300 NIS with you from the country you come with and exchange more money in Israel as need be. The best places to exchange your money are the bank and post office.

Hotels:

Hotels in Israel are very expensive, especially if you are looking in staying in hot spots and big cities.

For example, in the Jerusalem/Tel Aviv area, a night in a 3-star hotel could cost 200$.

How to Save money:

The best way to save money is by renting an apartment. There are many beautiful and cheap apartments in the country.

Another is sleeping in a Zimmer. A Zimmer is the Israeli version of Bed and breakfasts but generally smaller, more private, and often, more luxurious. Zimmers are owned privately and are sometimes located on the grounds of a family home.

Another way is sleeping in a city nearby. For example, if you're looking into visiting Tel Aviv, I would look for a hotel in either Ramat Gan or Givati'im which are both on the borders of Tel Aviv.

 

Restaurants:

Most restaurants in the country are expensive, a meal in a regular restaurant can cost $20 for just the main course and a drink per person.

How to save money:

Eat at food stands. Food stands are pretty common here and there is no need to be afraid to eat in the food stands in Israel because there is a lot of food control by the Ministry of Health in the country and so many people eat there that there is always food turnover.

 

Transportation:

Trains and taxis are pretty expensive in Israel. For example, a 15-minute ride in a taxi within the city can easily cost $20 and even 30$ in Tel-Aviv.

How to save money:

Sadly, we don't have Uber in the country, but it is pretty comfortable, safe, and cheap to get around by bus or light rail. on weekends there are also night lines that run until 4 AM in the big cities. There are also bicycles and scooters that you can rent around popular cities like Tel-Aviv.

Parks and Attractions:

Parks and attractions can cost around $7-15 depending on what you are planning on doing here.

How to save money:

Israel has 49 national parks, and 43 nature reserves. If you plan on going to many it is worth getting an annual subscription called 'Matmon' that costs about $40 for an entire year. It gives free admission to almost all the parks and discounts to some of the attractions.

There are also many free attractions in Israel that I will be talking about soon.

 

Shopping:

Buying clothes, shoes, cosmetics, and electronics are expensive in Israel, so rely on going on a shopping spree while visiting Israel.

How to save money:

In Eilat, there is no VAT and therefore there is 17% of the price compared to the rest of the country so if you must buy things in Israel I recommend you shop there.

 

Food shopping:

When staying in the center of the big cities you do not want to buy in the Mini Markets because prices can be 3 times more expensive than any other supermarket.

How to save money:

A great way to save money is to cook in your apartment. It is worth going a bit outside the city center to shop at a big supermarket. The cheap chains are 'Rami Levy' רמי לוי , 'Shufersal Deal' שופרסל דיל , 'Yochannoff' יוחננוף.

On top of that, we also recommend you visit the Shuk (market) that sells fresh fruits, vegetables, meat, and fish for the best price. Don't forget to bargain! It is popular in Israel.
