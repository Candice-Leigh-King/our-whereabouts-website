We almost skipped Seceda due to bad weather predictions. When we woke up, we were blessed with a beautiful day. Change of plans, HERE WE COME SECEDA! YEAH! It quickly became one of our favorite spots in the Dolomites because it has a panoramic view that definitely stands out has a unique view.

There are two ways to get up to Seceda, you could either hike up or take the cable car. I'm going to focus on the second option, cable car.

How to get there:

The cable car to Seceda is called 'Seceda Valley Station' and is located in Ortisei, Val Gardena

 

Parking:

Parking costs 1.5 Euro per hour or 8 Euro for the day.

When to visit:

Best months From June to September (depending on the weather that year, there may still be snow in May and it might start snowing as early as September). To avoid crowds, aim for September-October.

During the winter Seceda is used as a ski slope.

 

Cable car:

This is the best and easiest way to reach the top. You can purchase tickets from the ticket center or online. Opening times may vary due to weather conditions. Diligence requires you to check the cable car schedules before you go. From Ortisei, the cable car goes to Furnes and from there you have to change and take the Gondola to Seceda.

  • Price: 35 Euro per person return ticket, from Ortisei to Seceda and back (it includes both cable cars). If you'd like to take the Cable car up and hike down, a one-way ticket will cost you 25 Euro
  • Opening hours: Winter Season (December to mid-April): 8:30-16:30. Summer Season (end of May to mid-October): from 8:30-17:30

Trails:

We walked around while enjoying the view. The path is a circular trail that takes about 30 minutes if you don't stop to admire and take lots of beautiful photos.

  • Seceda Lookout point: Once you arrive at the cable car station, you can reach the panoramic viewpoint in just 10 minutes. There is a metal ring with the names of all the mountains on it, so you know what mountains you're seeing.
  • Pieralongia: We didn't do this hike, since it's about 2-3 hours and we were short with time, but I was told it's an easy 5km hike. It starts at the cable car station.

Food:

There are two restaurants on the slope called Baita Sofie Hütte and Baita Daniel Hütte. Baita Sofie Hütte is located not far from the Cable car station but Baita Daniel Hütte is 1.7km down hill. You can always sit down, relax and have a picnic. Please leave no trace.

 

Bathrooms:

There are bathrooms right as you get off the cable car and it's free!

 

Attire Essentials:

In the mountains, the weather can be unpredictable. Always pack layers and warm clothes, suitable hiking shoes, and a raincoat.
