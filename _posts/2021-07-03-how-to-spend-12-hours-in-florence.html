Since we had 8 full days in Rome we decided to go venture outside of the city. We decided to visit Florence since it is not too far from Rome and we absolutely fell in love with the capital city of Tuscany.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

WHEN TO VISIT:


The best time to visit is during the shoulder season when there are less tourists and the weather isn't too bad. The peak season is mid-June through August, shoulder season is April through mid-June and September through October. 
Florence weather in May is quite enjoyable with up to 11 hours of sunshine per day and 15 hours of daylight. The average temperature is 16°C, which is a little chilly so bring warm clothes with you.

TRAIN FROM ROME TO FLORENCE:

We decided to take the bullet train from Rome to Florence since it is faster than renting a car and driving to Florence. The bullet train goes up to 280 KPH. The earlier you book your tickets the cheaper it is. You could get tickets for real cheap if you buy them 4-6 months before your trip. We didn’t know so and booked our tickets 2 months in advance and that is why the price was so high. It is also important to buy the tickets back and forth.

  • Route: Termini Station (the station in Rome) to Santa Maria Novela (the stop in Florence).
  • Cost: 64 USD per person.
  • Tickets: Print out your tickets that way you get a bar code and just scan it through the machines and don’t have to wait in line to convert your order into tickets in the booths.
  • Comfort: The train has very comfortable seats and outlets to charge your phone so relax and enjoy the beautiful view of Tuscany as you pass them on the train.

WHEN YOU SHOULD GO:

Since it takes a 1 hour and 40 minutes train ride, we decided to take the 7 AM train so we could arrive at Piazzale Michelangelo at around 9 am and enjoy the beautiful view before the crowds come. We wanted to make the most out of our day in Florence so we decided to book the 9 pm train back to Rome so that gave us a great amount of time to enjoy the city.

 

TRANSPORTATION:

When you exit the train station in Florence you will see a machine on your left that you could buy bus tickets. It’s about 1.5$ for a bus fair for 90 minutes. We then crossed the street and took the a bus to Piazzale Michelangelo. The bus drops you off right there and from there we walked everywhere.
