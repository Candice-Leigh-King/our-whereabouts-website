Our first stop in the Dolomites was Lago di Carezza. Lago di Carezza, aka, Karersee in German, is a small alpine lake and is known for its wonderful colors and view of mount Latermar. It is one of the most beautiful lakes in the Dolomites and should be on your itinerary when visiting.

Some History:

This lake is about 240 million years old. The whole area was once under sea.

The lake is known as Rainbow lake because of it's beautiful colors and the legend behind the lake.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

The legend:

Once upon a time, there was a mermaid living in the lake. The wizard Masaré was in love with her. In order to seduce her, he followed the advice of the witch Lanwerda to dress up as a jewel merchant and to throw a rainbow between the Mt Catinaccio and Mt Latemar. The wizard did this, but he forgot to dress up as a jewel merchant. The beautiful mermaid realized that he was a wizard and disappeared in the lake. In sadness the wizard then threw the rainbow and all the jewels into the lake. That’s why the lake still features such beautiful colors.

Entrance Fee:

Free.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

When to visit:

In our opinion, the best time to visit Lago di Carezza is in shoulder season (April-May and September-October), when the weather is still warm, the water high enough, and isn't as populated.

Sunrise and sunset are both magical times to visit Lago di Carezza. We tried for sunset..... But the weather was horrible.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Parking:

The parking lot is pretty cheap and brings you straight to the beautiful small lake. The first 15 minutes are free, One hour costs 1 Euro, 2 hours costs 2 Euro, And 2-6 hours cost 4 Euro.

- _section-type: blog-window right_image: /uploads/20210908-175457.jpg left_image: /uploads/20210908-175858-1.jpg - _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

How to get there:

Obviously, the easiest way is by car but you could also take a bus from Bolzano to Val di Fassa and stop right at Lago di Carezza parking lot. How awesome is that?

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Walking around:

There's a small walking circular trail around the lake that takes about 20 minutes. It's a beautiful walk through the Latermar forest that surrounds the lake. It’s a easy walk with a few great lookout points to view from different angles.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Swimming:

Swimming here is not allowed since the lake is a protected natural landmark and fenced all around. Climbing the fence is prohibited. Why am I mentioning this? Many people climb the fence, take their awesome Instagram shots with the big rock without realizing this is prohibited and violators will be FINED!

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Drones:

It is prohibited to fly drones here.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Food:

There’s a restaurant and a café serving food, drinks, and souvenirs. The food and drinks were reasonably priced.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Bathrooms:

Public bathrooms are available in the tunnel linking the parkinglot to the lake.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Our photos don't do justice to how beautiful this place really is. We had bad weather and since we came from Venice where it was hot, we weren't prepared for this weather but trust me when I tell you that this lake must be on your list!
