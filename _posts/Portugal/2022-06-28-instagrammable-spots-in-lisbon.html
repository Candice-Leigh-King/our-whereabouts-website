The Algarve coast, located in southern Portugal, is a paradise for beach lovers and surfers, but did you know that there are also really great hikes along the coast? Here are a few that you shouldn't miss.

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Miradouro de Santa Luzia

 

This romantic terrace is located by the church of Santa Luzia and provides a beautiful view of Alfama district with a view over its houses, churches, and the Tagus River.
The best time to visit this beautiful viewpoint is during sunset. Sunrises here aren't the greatest time to visit for photography but are definitely beautiful and less crowded.

Carvoeiro Boardwalk and Algar Seco

  • Region: Lagoa, South Coast
  • Distance: 1km
  • Average duration: 1h

You can extend the Seven Hanging Valleys hike by walking further to the Algar Seco caves and Carvoeiro Boardwalk. If you're too tired you can always drive to Algar Seco and Carvoeiro Boardwalk by car.  They are both amazing places to explore.

- _section-type: blog-window right_image: /uploads/23.jpg left_image: /uploads/20220504-075814-2.jpg - _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

Ponta da Piedade

  • Region: Lagos, South Coast
  • Distance: 6km round trip
  • Average duration: 2h

Ponta da Piedade is a great headland with unique rock formations, and arches sculpted by wind and waves over thousands of years. While here, don't miss visiting the picturesque cove at the base of Ponta da Piedade, which can be accessed via a set of stairs. You can take a boat or a kayak tour to get a closer look at all the arches and grottoes.

- _section-type: blog-cover-image blog-cover_image: /uploads/20220502-123644.jpg - _section-type: blog-featured-textarea blog-featured-textarea_html: >-

Trail of Headlands 

  • Trailhead: Praia dos Caneiros or Praia do Carvoeiro
  • Distance: 6 km.
  • Duration: 2 hours.

The trail of the headlands is a great alternative to the Seven Hanging Valleys Trail. It’s not as known, so there are fewer people on the trail. You can do it either back & forth which would be 12km or you can do it only one way 6 km and then get a taxi/uber back to the starting point of the hike. The trail affords you spectacular views of cliffs and hidden coves.
