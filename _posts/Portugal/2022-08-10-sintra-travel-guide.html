​​Planning a trip to Sintra, Portugal? In this post, I cover when to visit, how long to stay,  what to pack, how to get there, the main attractions, and some of our tips.

Sintra is a charming Portuguese town located in the Sintra mountains near Lisbon. Its natural beauty and architecture, are the reasons why it's classified by UNESCO as a World Heritage site.

HISTORY OF SINTRA:

In the 19th century Sintra became the first centre of European Romantic architecture. Ferdinand II turned a ruined monastery into a castle where this new sensitivity was displayed in the use of Gothic, Egyptian, Moorish and Renaissance elements and in the creation of a park blending local and exotic species of trees.

 

HOW TO GET THERE:

There are a few ways to get to Sintra

  • Train: This is the most convenient and inexpensive way of reaching Sintra from Lisbon. You take the train from the city's central Rossio railway station.
    The train departures daily every 15 minutes, on Saturday and Sunday they run hourly.
    Duration: 40 minutes
    Cost: 4.3 EUR.
  • Buses: Do know there aren't direct buses from Lisbon to Sintra.
  • Car: You can always drive to Sintra but do know that parking is minimal and the roads are very narrow you can get stuck in still traffic most of the day.
  • Tour: There are many tours from Lisbon to Sintra that includes various spots around Sintra.
    Cost: Prices start at 61$ per person and could go up to 200$

WHEN TO VISIT:

The peak season is July-August. Sintra is very popular due to its proximity to Lisbon and its charm, in order to truly enjoy it, we recommend visiting April-June, September-November.
Try to avoid weekends in order to beat the crowds.

HOW LONG TO STAY: 

Most people visit Sintra on a day trip from Lisbon, which is what we did.
We spent a whole full day in Sintra but also got to enjoy part of what Sintra has to offer.
If we could, we'd stay in Sintra overnight and spend at least 2 days here in order to explore all 5 palaces and the many gardens.

HOW TO GET AROUND SINTRA:

Since we don't recommend driving in Sintra, the most affordable way to get around is by taking the buses or Uber. There are a few ways to get around Sintra:

  • Buses: 

    Bus 434 circular route: Sintra Train Station - Historic Center - Moorish Castle - Pena Palace and Park - Historic Center - Sintra Train Station
    Bus 435: Monserrate Palace, Seteais Palace, and Quinta da Regaleira. The start and end point is at the historic center. This line runs every 25 minutes.
    The tickets for both buses can be purchased directly from the driver.
    Cost: 3.5 EUR.

  • Tuk-tuk: There are always so many Tuk-tuks around Sintra, the problem is they don't have a set rate and each driver gives a different price.

  • Taxis: They are metered taxis. From Quinta da Regaleira to the train station it cost at least €10 and probably more if there is lots of traffic.

  • Uber: The uber drivers are located at the train station of Sintra (which is about 15 minutes away from the palaces) once you order one from the palaces it can take them 15-20 minutes to arrive to you, and sometimes they might accept your ride and then cancel it midway.

  • Walking: Sintra is small enough that many area attractions can be accessed on foot. If you plan your trip correctly, you can mix up public transportation with walking since some spots are within walking distance of each other. For exmaple: Pena Palace & Moorish Castle, National Palace of Sintra, Quinta da Regaleira & Monserrate Palace

 

WHAT TO PACK:

  • Snacks and food
  • Water
  • Hat
  • Sunglasses
  • An extra layer since it gets chilly in the early morning/late evening.
  • Comfortable shoes
  • Sunscreen

FOOD:

We were told that there isn't much to eat there but they do have Kiyosks and coffee shops at the palaces, the center of town, and by the train station of Sintra.

  • Pena Palace: Palace Restaurant, Palace Cafeteria, Terrace Bar, and Riding Ring Kiosk

  • Moorish Castle: There is a little cafe near the entrance with drinks, snacks and sandwiches. 

  • National Palace of Sintra: Robillion Pavillion Cafeteria 

  •  

    Monserrate Palace - Monserrate's Tea House

TIPS:

  • Plan ahead of time which places you want to see.
  • Don't visit more than 3 palaces for a day.
  • Start with the most popular spots.
  • The first and last hours of the day is the least busy time.
  • Purchase combo Tickets: If you're planning on visiting a few palaces, buy a combo ticket for them, they are cheaper that way.

MAIN ATTRACTIONS:

  • Old Town of Sinta
  • National Palace of Sintra
  • Pena Palace
  • Monserrate Palace
  • Moorish Castle
  • Quinta  da Regaleira
