As you scroll through social media and you see all these beautiful shots people take with these unique and stunning bridges one might think that they are all noticeable and easy to find. Well that is not the case. Did you know that there are 400 bridges in Venice? Yes, that is a large amount of bridges. I'm here to help you out with find these beautiful bridges.

I added a map that I created here of the distracts in Venice so it could help you.

CALLE DRIO CHIESA BRIDGE

District: San Marco.
Location: Near Teatro La Fenice.

Enter 'Hotel San Moise' into your google maps. Once you see the hotel on your right you'll find a small dock on your left where this photo was taken. Not many people know about this spot and the location of it makes it invisible to those who aren't aware of this spot.

Coordinates:

45.433443, 12.335059

PONTE MARIA CALLAS BRIDGE

District: San Marco.
Location: Near Teatro La Fenice

This beautiful bridge on a very small canal is hidden between the buildings, which creates a very cozy and picturesque spot. What adds even more charm to it is the Santo Stefano bell tower behind.

PONTE BALBI

District: San Marco.
Location: Near Ponte Rialto.

This is a really cute bridge located near Venchi Ice Cream shop. It isn't as busy as others and it is very charming with colorful houses all around it.

PONTE DEI SOPIRI

District: San Marco.
Location: Near Basilica di San Marco.

There are four different angles where you can capture a photo with this bridge:

Option 1: Ponte della Paglia: It's a bridge right next to St. Mark's square and just behind the Doge's Palace.

Option 2: Below Ponte della Pagila when facing the Bridge of Sighs walk to your right off the bridge and make a left turn to an alley for this cool spot.

Option 3: The bridge near Trattoria Canonica‏ restaurant.

Option 4: Gondola ride. This is a great option, but an expensive one. (Most expensive option..)

PONTE DEL PARADISO

District: Castello.
Location: Near Campo S. M. Formosa.

Crossing the Rio del Mondo Novo, the bridge is almost entirely constructed out of Istrian stone bricks while the steps are paved with Trachyte. As you reach the bridge you'll notice a arch connected by the side buildings known as the arch of Madonna della Misericordia.

PONTE PASQUALIGO E AVOGADRO

District: Castello.
Location: Near Fondazione Querini Stampalia Museum.

This is a very charming area that we happened to find while getting lost. It has a few really neat bridges and balconies that is worth checking out.

Coordinates:

45.436508, 12.340923

PONTE RIALTO

District: Connects San Marco and San Polo. Location: Near Church of San Bartolomeo di Rialto.

One of the most famous bridges in Venice and for a good reason. It is the first bridge built across the Gran Canal which makes it also the oldest one out of only 4 bridges that are crossing this main canal of Venice. 3 ways of photographing this bridge:

Option 1: On the San Marco side there are a few docks on the Gran Canal but the best one is closest to the bridge.

Option 2: On the San Polo side you'll find a cute little spot in front of Palazzo dei Camerlenghi.

Coordinates for this spot in photo:

45.4384198, 12.3359621.

Option 3: Gondola ride. This is a great option, but an expensive one. (Most expensive option..)
