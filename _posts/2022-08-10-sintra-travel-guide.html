​​Planning a trip to Sintra, Portugal? In this post, I cover when to visit, how long to stay,  what to pack, how to get there, the main attractions, and some of our tips.

Sintra is a charming Portuguese town located in the Sintra mountains near Lisbon. Its natural beauty and architercutre, are the reasons why it's classified by UNESCO as a World Heritage site. 

 

- _section-type: blog-textarea textarea_html: >-

HISTORY OF SINTRA:

In the 19th century Sintra became the first centre of European Romantic architecture. Ferdinand II turned a ruined monastery into a castle where this new sensitivity was displayed in the use of Gothic, Egyptian, Moorish and Renaissance elements and in the creation of a park blending local and exotic species of trees.

HOW TO GET THERE:

There are a few ways to get to Sintra

  • Train: This is the most convenient and inexpensive way of reaching Sintra from Lisbon. You take the train from the city's central Rossio railway station.
    The train departures daily every 15 minutes, on Saturday and Sunday they run hourly.
    Duration: 40 minutes
    Cost: 4.3 EUR.
  • Buses: Do know there aren't direct buses from Lisbon to Sintra.
  • Car: You can always drive to Sintra but do know that parking is minimal and the roads are very narrow you can get stuck in still traffic most of the day.
  • Tour: There are many tours from Lisbon to Sintra that includes various spots around Sintra.
    Cost: Prices start at 61$ per person and could go up to 200$

WHEN TO VISIT:

The peak season is July-August. Sintra is very popular due to its proximity to Lisbon and its charm, in order to truly enjoy it, we recommend visiting April-June, September-November.
Try to avoid weekends in order to beat the crowds.

HOW LONG TO STAY: 

Most people visit Sintra on a day trip from Lisbon, which is what we did.
We spent a whole full day in Sintra but also got to enjoy part of what Sintra has to offer.
If we could, we'd stay in Sintra overnight and spend at least 2 days here in order to explore all 5 palaces and the many gardens.

WHAT TO PACK:

  • Snacks and food
  • Water
  • Hat
  • Sunglasses
  • An extra layer since it gets chilly in the early morning/late evening.
  • Comfortable shoes
  • Sunscreen
